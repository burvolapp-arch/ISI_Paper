\section{Classification Thresholds}\label{sec:appendix:thresholds}

\begin{table}[htbp]
  \centering\small
  \caption{Classification thresholds (reproduced from
    \cref{tab:thresholds}).}\label{tab:appendix-thresholds}
  \begin{tabular}{@{}l l l@{}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Tier} & \textbf{Composite range} & \textbf{Label} \\
    \midrule
    1 & \(\mathrm{ISI}_i \geq 0.50\)          & \texttt{highly\_concentrated} \\
    2 & \(0.25 \leq \mathrm{ISI}_i < 0.50\)   & \texttt{moderately\_concentrated} \\
    3 & \(0.15 \leq \mathrm{ISI}_i < 0.25\)   & \texttt{mildly\_concentrated} \\
    4 & \(\mathrm{ISI}_i < 0.15\)              & \texttt{unconcentrated} \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

The thresholds are adapted from the US~DOJ/FTC Horizontal Merger Guidelines
\parencite{doj2023merger}, which classify markets as highly concentrated
(\HHI above \num{2500}), moderately concentrated (\HHI \num{1500}--\num{2500}),
and unconcentrated (\HHI below \num{1500}) on the \num{0}--\num{10000} scale.
The \ISI thresholds represent a proportional transposition to the unit interval,
with an additional tier boundary at \num{0.15} to separate mildly concentrated
from unconcentrated states.
