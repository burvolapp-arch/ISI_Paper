\begin{abstract}
\noindent
This paper reports the first empirical application of the International
Sovereignty Index (ISI) to the 27~member states of the European Union
using vintage-2024 data under methodology v1.0.  The ISI composite,
defined as the unweighted arithmetic mean of six normalised axes
(financial, energy, technology, defence, critical inputs, logistics),
ranges from \num{0.23567126} (France, rank~27) to \num{0.51748148}
(Malta, rank~1) with a population mean of \num{0.34439144} and
population standard deviation of \num{0.06992188}.  One country is
classified as \emph{highly concentrated}, 23~as \emph{moderately
concentrated}, and three as \emph{mildly concentrated}.
A variance decomposition shows that defence (35.1\%) and logistics
(34.3\%) together account for 69.4\% of cross-country composite
variance.  Leave-one-axis-out analysis confirms that removing the
defence axis produces the largest rank disruption (Spearman $\rho =
0.833$).  Dirichlet weight perturbation (10\,000 draws, $\alpha_j=10$)
yields a mean rank-order correlation of $\rho = 0.979$, with the
Netherlands exhibiting the highest rank volatility ($\sigma = 3.07$).
Z-score standardisation produces $\rho = 0.896$ against the baseline,
indicating moderate sensitivity to implicit axis scaling.
Winsorisation of the two highest-variance axes at the 95th percentile
preserves the full rank order ($\rho = 1.000$).  The paper provides
radar profiles for eight selected countries, full axis-level rankings,
and a complete reproducibility protocol.
\end{abstract}
